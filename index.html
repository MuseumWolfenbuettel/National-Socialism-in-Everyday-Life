20.National Socialism in Everyday Life

The Nazification of public administration in Wolfenbüttel began on November 4, 1933, with the appointment of Fritz Ramien, a member of the NSDAP since 1932, as mayor. However, the most powerful figure in Wolfenbüttel became NSDAP district leader Kurt Bertram, who, like his successors, enforced the interests of Hitler's party throughout the district and reported directly to the Gauleiter. Parades and public assemblies, organized for new holidays such as the anniversary of the Ludendorff-Hitler Putsch on November 9 or the day Hitler assumed the chancellorship, dominated the streets and event calendars. Nazi propaganda infiltrated all areas of life in Wolfenbüttel. A multitude of National Socialist organizations influenced the town's residents. Youth were indoctrinated into National Socialism through the Hitler Youth (HJ), the League of German Girls (BDM), and the Deutsches Jungvolk (DJ). The Law on the Hitler Youth, enacted in late 1936, made these groups the only state-recognized youth organizations, with nearly 98 percent of all German youth soon joining them. Alongside leisure activities, mandatory programs included “physical training” and ideological indoctrination. Key educational goals focused on instilling command and obedience, camaraderie, discipline, and self-sacrifice for the “national community.” Everyday life became increasingly militarized as the population was prepared for war. Field exercises and roll calls shaped the daily experiences of many young people, embedding militaristic values into their upbringing.
